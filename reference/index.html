A compact summary of notation, key results, and textbook references for the complex analysis course.

Key Results

Euler's Formula. $e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$

Cauchy–Riemann Equations. $f = u + iv$ is analytic iff $\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \dfrac{\partial v}{\partial y}$ and $\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial x}$.

Cauchy's Integral Formula. If $f$ is analytic inside and on a simple closed contour $\gamma$, then $$f(a) = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{z - a}\,dz$$

Cauchy's Integral Formula for Derivatives. $$f^{(n)}(a) = \frac{n!}{2\pi i}\oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{(z-a)^{n+1}}\,dz$$

Residue Theorem. If $f$ is analytic inside $\gamma$ except at isolated singularities $a_1, \ldots, a_n$, then $$\oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i \sum_{k=1}^{n} n(\gamma, a_k)\operatorname{Res}_{z=a_k} f$$

ML Inequality. If $|f(z)| \le M$ on $\gamma$ and $\text{length}(\gamma) = L$, then $\left|\oint_\gamma f\,dz\right| \le ML$.

Liouville's Theorem. A bounded entire function is constant.

Maximum Modulus Principle. If $f$ is analytic and non-constant on a domain $\Omega$, then $|f|$ has no local maximum in $\Omega$.

Taylor's Theorem. If $f$ is analytic in $D(a, R)$, then $f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(z-a)^n$ for $|z-a| < R$.

Laurent's Theorem. In the annulus $r < |z-a| < R$, $f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} c_n(z-a)^n$ where $c_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint \frac{f(\zeta)}{(\zeta - a)^{n+1}}\,d\zeta$.

Argument Principle. If $f$ is meromorphic inside $\gamma$ with $Z$ zeros and $P$ poles (counted with multiplicity), then $\frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint_\gamma \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)}\,dz = Z - P$.

Rouché's Theorem. If $|g(z)| < |f(z)|$ on $\gamma$, then $f$ and $f + g$ have the same number of zeros inside $\gamma$.

Schwarz Lemma. If $f:\mathbb{D}\to\mathbb{D}$ is analytic with $f(0)=0$, then $|f(z)|\le|z|$ and $|f'(0)|\le 1$.

Riemann Mapping Theorem. Every simply connected proper subdomain of $\mathbb{C}$ is conformally equivalent to $\mathbb{D}$.

Topics by Chapter

ChapterTopics
1. Complex Numbers Rectangular & polar forms, Euler's formula, roots of unity, triangle inequality, topology of $\mathbb{C}$
2. Analytic Functions Differentiability, Cauchy–Riemann equations, harmonic functions, elementary functions, branch cuts
3. Complex Integration Contour integrals, Cauchy–Goursat theorem, Cauchy integral formula, winding numbers, ML inequality
4. Series & Residues Taylor & Laurent series, singularity classification, residue theorem, real integral computation
5. Conformal Mappings Möbius transformations, cross-ratio, Schwarz lemma, Joukowski map, Riemann mapping theorem
6. Applications Potential flow, electrostatics, Julia sets, Riemann surfaces

Mathematical Prerequisites

  • Single-variable calculus (derivatives, integrals, series)
  • Multivariable calculus (partial derivatives, line integrals, Green's theorem)
  • Linear algebra basics (matrices, determinants)
  • Real analysis concepts (sequences, limits, continuity, uniform convergence)
  • Basic topology ($\mathbb{R}^2$: open/closed sets, compactness, connectedness)

References & Textbooks

Complex Analysis
Elias M. Stein & Rami Shakarchi
Princeton Lectures in Analysis II, Princeton University Press, 2003.
Rigorous yet accessible. Excellent treatment of Cauchy's theory and conformal mappings.
Complex Variables and Applications
James Ward Brown & Ruel V. Churchill
9th Edition, McGraw-Hill, 2013.
The standard engineering-oriented textbook. Rich in worked examples and applications.
Fundamentals of Complex Analysis
Edward B. Saff & Arthur D. Snider
3rd Edition, Pearson, 2003.
Application-focused with excellent problems on fluid flow and electrostatics.
Complex Analysis
Lars V. Ahlfors
3rd Edition, McGraw-Hill, 1979.
The classic graduate text. Geometric viewpoint throughout; proves Riemann mapping theorem elegantly.
Visual Complex Analysis
Tristan Needham
Oxford University Press, 1997.
A deeply geometric and visual approach — the spiritual ancestor of this interactive site.
