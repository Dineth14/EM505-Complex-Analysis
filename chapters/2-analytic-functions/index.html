Chapter 2

Analytic Functions

The derivative of a complex function is far more restrictive than its real counterpart. Functions that are complex-differentiable — analytic functions — satisfy the Cauchy–Riemann equations and possess extraordinary properties.

2.1 Complex Differentiability

A function $f: U \to \mathbb{C}$ is complex differentiable (holomorphic) at $z_0 \in U$ if the limit $$f'(z_0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(z_0 + h) - f(z_0)}{h}$$ exists, where $h \in \mathbb{C}$ may approach $0$ from any direction.

The key difference from real differentiation: $h$ is complex, so the limit must be the same regardless of the direction of approach. This imposes strong constraints.

2.2 Cauchy–Riemann Equations

Write $f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y)$ where $z = x + iy$. Then $f$ is analytic if and only if $u$ and $v$ have continuous partial derivatives satisfying:

(Cauchy–Riemann Equations) $$\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \qquad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}$$

If $f = u + iv$ is analytic, then both $u$ and $v$ are harmonic: $\nabla^2 u = u_{xx} + u_{yy} = 0$ and $\nabla^2 v = 0$. Moreover, $v$ is called the harmonic conjugate of $u$.

2.4 Visualizing the Cauchy–Riemann Equations

2.6 Elementary Complex Functions

The complex exponential is $e^z = e^x(\cos y + i \sin y)$, which is entire (analytic everywhere) and periodic with period $2\pi i$.

The complex logarithm is multi-valued: $\log z = \ln|z| + i(\arg z + 2\pi k)$ for $k \in \mathbb{Z}$. The principal branch uses $-\pi < \arg z \le \pi$. The branch cut runs along the negative real axis.

(Identity Theorem) If $f$ and $g$ are analytic on a domain $D$ and $f(z_n) = g(z_n)$ for a sequence $\{z_n\}$ converging to a point in $D$, then $f \equiv g$ on all of $D$.

Key Takeaways

Cauchy–Riemann

$u_x = v_y$ and $u_y = -v_x$ — the gateway to analyticity.

Domain Coloring

Hue = argument, brightness = modulus. Zeros and poles are visible as color vortices.

Conformality

Analytic functions with $f'(z)\neq 0$ preserve angles between curves.

Rigidity

Analytic functions are completely determined by their values on any convergent sequence.
