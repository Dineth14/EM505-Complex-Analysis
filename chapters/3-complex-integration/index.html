Chapter 3

Complex Integration

Integration in the complex plane is profoundly different from real integration. The path matters — unless the function is analytic, in which case Cauchy's theorem grants extraordinary freedom.

3.1 Contour Integrals

Let $\gamma: [a,b] \to \mathbb{C}$ be a smooth curve and $f$ continuous on $\gamma$. The contour integral of $f$ along $\gamma$ is $$\int_\gamma f(z)\,dz = \int_a^b f(\gamma(t))\,\gamma'(t)\,dt$$

Key properties: linearity, reversal of orientation negates the integral, and the ML inequality gives an upper bound:

$$\left|\int_\gamma f(z)\,dz\right| \le M \cdot L(\gamma)$$ where $M = \max_{z \in \gamma}|f(z)|$ and $L(\gamma)$ is the arc length.

3.2 Cauchy–Goursat Theorem

(Cauchy–Goursat) If $f$ is analytic on and inside a simple closed contour $\gamma$, then $$\oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 0$$

This is one of the most important results in all of mathematics. It says that for analytic functions, closed contour integrals vanish — the integral depends only on the endpoints, not the path.

3.3 Cauchy Integral Formula

(Cauchy Integral Formula) If $f$ is analytic inside and on a simple closed contour $\gamma$, and $z_0$ is inside $\gamma$, then $$f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{z - z_0}\,dz$$ Moreover, the derivatives satisfy $$f^{(n)}(z_0) = \frac{n!}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{(z - z_0)^{n+1}}\,dz$$

This remarkable formula says an analytic function's value at any interior point is completely determined by its values on the boundary. It also proves that analytic functions are infinitely differentiable.

3.4 Winding Numbers

The winding number (or index) of a closed curve $\gamma$ about a point $z_0 \notin \gamma$ is $$n(\gamma, z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{dz}{z - z_0} \in \mathbb{Z}$$ It counts the number of times $\gamma$ winds around $z_0$ counter-clockwise.

Key Takeaways

Cauchy–Goursat

$\oint_\gamma f\,dz = 0$ for analytic $f$ on simply connected domains.

Integral Formula

Boundary values of $f$ determine interior values — and all derivatives.

Winding Numbers

$n(\gamma,z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint \frac{dz}{z-z_0}$ counts loops around $z_0$.

ML Inequality

$|\int_\gamma f\,dz| \le M \cdot L$ — a universal bound on contour integrals.
