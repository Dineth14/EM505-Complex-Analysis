Applications

Complex analysis has stunning applications to fluid dynamics, electrostatics, fractal geometry, and the visualization of multi-valued functions via Riemann surfaces.

6.1 Potential Flow

In 2D incompressible irrotational flow, the velocity field can be derived from a complex potential $\Phi(z) = \phi(x,y) + i\psi(x,y)$ where $\phi$ is the velocity potential and $\psi$ the stream function. Level curves of $\psi$ are streamlines.

$$\text{Velocity: } \overline{V} = \frac{d\Phi}{dz} = u - iv$$

Common potentials:

  • Uniform flow: $\Phi = Uz$ (velocity $U$ in the $x$-direction)
  • Source/sink: $\Phi = \frac{m}{2\pi}\log z$ (source of strength $m$)
  • Vortex: $\Phi = \frac{-i\Gamma}{2\pi}\log z$ (circulation $\Gamma$)
  • Flow around cylinder: $\Phi = U(z + a^2/z)$

6.2 Julia Sets

For a complex polynomial $f_c(z) = z^2 + c$, the Julia set $J_c$ is the boundary of the set of points whose orbits remain bounded under iteration. When $c$ is in the Mandelbrot set, $J_c$ is connected; otherwise it is a Cantor dust.

6.3 Riemann Surfaces

Multi-valued functions like $\sqrt{z}$ and $\log z$ become single-valued on their Riemann surfaces. For $w = \sqrt{z}$, the Riemann surface is a two-sheeted surface with a branch point at the origin, connected by a branch cut.
