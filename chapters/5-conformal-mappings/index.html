Chapter 5

Conformal Mappings

Conformal mappings preserve angles and local shape. Möbius transformations, the Joukowski map, and the Riemann mapping theorem are cornerstones of geometric function theory with deep applications.

5.1 Möbius Transformations

A Möbius transformation (linear fractional transformation) has the form $$T(z) = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}, \qquad ad - bc \neq 0$$ where $a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{C}$. They map circles and lines to circles and lines, and form a group under composition.

A Möbius transformation is uniquely determined by three points and their images: given $z_1 \mapsto w_1$, $z_2 \mapsto w_2$, $z_3 \mapsto w_3$, there is exactly one Möbius transformation $T$ with these properties. The cross-ratio $$(z, z_1; z_2, z_3) = \frac{(z - z_1)(z_2 - z_3)}{(z - z_3)(z_2 - z_1)}$$ is preserved by Möbius transformations.

5.2 Important Conformal Maps

(Schwarz Lemma) If $f: \mathbb{D} \to \mathbb{D}$ is analytic with $f(0) = 0$, then $|f(z)| \le |z|$ and $|f'(0)| \le 1$. Equality holds iff $f$ is a rotation: $f(z) = e^{i\theta}z$.

(Riemann Mapping Theorem) Every simply connected domain in $\mathbb{C}$ that is not all of $\mathbb{C}$ is conformally equivalent to the open unit disk $\mathbb{D}$. The mapping is unique up to a Möbius transformation of $\mathbb{D}$.

5.3 The Joukowski Transform

The Joukowski (Zhukowsky) transformation $$w = z + \frac{1}{z}$$ maps circles passing near the point $z = -1$ into airfoil shapes. It is the foundation of classical aerodynamics.

Key Takeaways

Möbius Transforms

$(az+b)/(cz+d)$ maps circles to circles. Three points determine it uniquely.

Schwarz Lemma

Disk self-maps fixing the origin are contractions — equality forces a rotation.

Riemann Mapping

Any simply connected proper subdomain of $\mathbb{C}$ is conformally a disk.

Joukowski Map

$z + 1/z$ transforms circles into airfoils — the birth of aerodynamic theory.
