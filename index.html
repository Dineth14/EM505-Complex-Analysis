What is this?

This is a fully interactive, browser-based textbook companion for Complex Analysis (also known as the theory of functions of a complex variable). Every major concept — from the geometry of complex arithmetic to the residue theorem and conformal mappings — comes with real-time simulations you can manipulate, pause, and explore at your own pace.

The site is designed for undergraduate mathematics, physics, and engineering students taking a first course in complex analysis. No server or build step is required: every visualization runs directly in your browser using Canvas 2D, Three.js for 3D surfaces, and KaTeX for beautiful mathematical typesetting.

Each chapter pairs rigorous definitions and theorems with interactive explorations. Drag points on the complex plane, watch Taylor series converge term by term, explore domain colorings of analytic functions, and build geometric intuition for Cauchy's integral formula — all without leaving your browser.
